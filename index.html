Short Bio


I am a Lecturer (Research & Education) in Computer Science at University of Exeter, UK.
In recent years, the continuous launch of satellites and the subsequent release of Earth Observation (EO) data, along with the creation of open benchmarking LiDAR databases, have provided vast amounts of information about Earth. My research focuses on realising the potential of these datasets by developing algorithms, pipelines, and tools primarily for forest monitoring.
I have implemented two versatile tools for generating feature vectors for machine learning applications: DASOS (https://github.com/Art-n-MathS/DASOS), included in the GitHub Arctic Vault, manages both full-waveform and discrete LiDAR, addressing variations in point cloud density; and PlotToSat (https://github.com/Art-n-MathS/PlotToSat ) offers a practical, time-efficient way to extract time-series from multi-source EO data across numerous plot regions spread across a landscape.
In previous work, I introduced methods for extracting structural elements from 3D windows to detect dead standing Eucalypt trees without tree delineation in low-density LiDAR data. I also proposed a new category of data structures, integral trees, for efficient LiDAR data management during 3D polygonal model creation. Additionally, I demonstrated that the SAR phenological cycle of the Paphos forest in Cyprus contains two peaks: the summer peak is associated with pine needle regeneration, and a February trough is potentially linked to the activity of the pityocampa pest.
